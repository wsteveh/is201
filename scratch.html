What is Doggonomics?

Economics shapes everything — housing prices, your salary, the cost of groceries, how companies compete. But most people never encounter it outside of a dry textbook. Doggonomics is my attempt to fix that.

I'm Steven Wood, an Economics and Mathematics student at BYU with a passion for making rigorous ideas accessible. On Doggonomics, I break down real economic concepts — from supply and demand to causal inference — in a way that's engaging for anyone, whether you've taken economics before or never thought about it at all.

Topics I Cover

The channel spans the big ideas in economics, organized into four main areas:

  1. Microeconomics
    • Supply, demand, and market equilibrium
    • Elasticity and consumer behavior
    • Firm competition: perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly
    • Game theory and strategic interaction
  2. Macroeconomics
    • GDP, inflation, and unemployment
    • Monetary policy and central banking
    • Fiscal policy and government spending
    • Business cycles and recessions
  3. Applied & Empirical Economics
    • Housing markets and real estate economics
    • Labor markets and wage determination
    • Causal inference — how we know what causes what
    • Reading and understanding economic data
  4. Industrial Organization
    • How firms compete in markets with few players
    • Entry strategy and market structure
    • Pricing strategies and product differentiation
