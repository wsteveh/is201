About Me

Economist. Founder. Educator. Perpetual learner.

My Story

I'm Steven Wood — an economics and mathematics student at Brigham Young University with a deep interest in how markets work, how data reveals truth, and how technology can amplify both. My academic journey has taken me from Utah State and BYU–Idaho to BYU Provo, where I'm completing a double major while maintaining a 4.0 GPA and conducting original research alongside faculty in the Strategy Department.

Outside the classroom, I'm the founder of LifeTrack, a web productivity app I built from scratch in Angular and launched on Google Firebase — complete with subscription payments. That project taught me more about shipping real software and understanding real customers than any class could.

I also run Doggonomics, a YouTube channel where I make economics accessible and interesting. It's my way of combining the analytical side of what I study with a genuine desire to share ideas that matter.

What Drives Me
Curiosity about causality — I'm fascinated by what actually causes things, not just correlation. Whether it's housing markets post-COVID or entry strategy theory, I want to know the mechanism.
Making ideas accessible — Economics is often locked behind jargon. Doggonomics exists because I believe anyone can understand these ideas if they're explained well.
Building things — I love the arc from idea to shipped product. LifeTrack started as a personal tool and became a real business. That process — the design, the code, the iteration — is something I genuinely enjoy.
